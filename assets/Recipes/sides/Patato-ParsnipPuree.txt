POTATO-PARSNIP PUREE

Test Kitchen Favorite
The dried porcim mushrooms are the secret to the exceptionally rich taste of this dish.

Prep time: 25 minutes
Cooking time: 30 minutes
EASY

2 1/2 pounds parsnips, peeled and sliced
1 can (14 1/2 oz.) chicken broth
Water
2 1/2 pounds Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled and cut into 1 1/2-inch chunks
1 package (0.35 oz.) dried porcini mushrooms, rinsed well
8 tablespoons butter, divided
1 1/2 pounds leeks, trimmed, white part only, rinsed well and chopped
1 teaspoon salt, divided


Per serving: 350 calories, 12,5 g total fat, 7.5 g saturat-ed fat, 31 mg cholesterol, 659 mg sodium, 
55 g carbo-hydrates, 5 g protein, 77 mg calcium, 10 g fiber

1. Bring parsnips, chicken broth and
enough water to fill the broth can to
a boil in Dutch oven. Reduce heat
and cook at a low boil, 5 minutes.
Add potatoes; cover and cook
20 minutes until vegetables are ten-
der. Drain vegetables in colander set
over a bowl. Reserve 1 V4 cups broth.

2. Pour 1/2 cup boiling water over
porcini in bowl; let stand 15 minutes.
Drain, reserving !/i cup liquid, and
mince. Add liquid to reserved broth.

3. Melt 3 tablespoons butter in the
same pot; add leeks and cook over
medium heat, 7 minutes. Add porcim
and V2 teaspoon salt; cover and cook
3 minutes more, until tender.

4. Coarsely mash potato-parsnip mix-
ture with a spoon; transfer half the
mixture to a food processor. Add half
of the reserved broth and butter. Pro-
cess, pulsing until smooth. Stir into
pot with leek mixture. Repeat with
remaining potato-parsnip mixture,
broth, butter and salt. (Can be made
ahead. Transfer to a microwaveproqf bowl.
Cover and refrigerate overnight. Micro-
wave on High 8 to 10 minutes, stirring
every 2 to 3 minutes, until hot.) Makes
8 servings.
